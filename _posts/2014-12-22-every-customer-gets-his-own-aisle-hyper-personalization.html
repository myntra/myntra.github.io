Let me start with a ridiculous question. How is buying fashion different from buying train tickets? Well, today you can buy both online – isn’t it? Indeed. But, wonder why fashion e-tailing lagged behind online ticketing systems by more than a decade? Think what’s different in the offline scenarios. Remember those long queues at train ticket counters – thank lord they are a thing of the past! One computer can easily serve more people than a thousand ticket counters. A couple of quick questions are all that you need to answer to get a ticket. Thus, ticketing is a natural problem to service online. Contrast this with your experience at your favorite fashion outlet. You don’t need to beat long queues to get into the store but the salesperson asks you a hundred questions before (s)he can get you the product that you need. Smarter salespeople ask lesser questions. Instead, they keep scanning you – your reaction to products that you see, the stuff that you are currently wearing, the expensive mobile phone that you are carrying … every bit is crucial to pick that perfect product for you. Effective fashion e-tailing requires technology to match what goes on in the mind of the brightest of sales-reps, i.e., understand the personal style of the customer and throw up products that suit his/her taste.

Personalizing Product Listings


Product listings in an e-commerce store are equivalent to aisles in a brick and mortar store. In fashion retail, organizing aisles and product racks is especially important since product discovery always starts there. You probably decide the model of the mobile phone that you purchase even before you walk into a store but you discover your t-shirts only while browsing through product racks. At Myntra, we focus a lot of our attention on product listings to ensure that the products that have the best chance of selling are featured at the top slots. At the same time, we give a fair chance to new products so that they can turn into best sellers.

A personalized product listing is a ranking of products generated for a customer that takes the following factors into consideration:
  • Personal Style / Product Taste: We profile everything that the customer browses or purchases in order to estimate his/her preferences toward different product attributes (e.g., brand, pattern, fabric, color, price points).
  • Current Intent: What is it that the customer looking for today?
  • Product Popularity: A product that sells more is likely to be ranked higher. In particular, we assess whether others who have a similar taste as that of the customer like the product.
